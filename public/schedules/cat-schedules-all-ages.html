Newborn (0–4 weeks)

Entirely mom-dependent. Kittens are blind, deaf, and cannot regulate their own body temperature or eliminate without stimulation. Your role is monitoring — not scheduling.

🐣 Key Notes Fading Kitten Syndrome can strike fast — call your vet immediately if any kitten is cold, unresponsive, crying constantly, or not gaining weight. The socialization window is 2–9 weeks: the most important period in a cat's life for human bonding. Gentle handling now = a friendlier cat forever.

🌱 Key Notes Spay/neuter is typically recommended around 4–5 months — before first heat in females. This dramatically reduces health risks and behavioral issues. Teething happens 3–6 months; redirect biting to toys, never hands. Start nail trims now — weekly touch of paws builds tolerance.

🐈 Key Notes The bedtime play session is the single most effective tool for reducing nighttime disruption — don't skip it. 🐱 Key Notes Cats are obligate carnivores — quality of food matters enormously. Wet food is strongly preferred over dry for hydration and kidney health. Annual vet checkups catch issues early; dental disease is the most common and preventable problem. Keep the litter box scrupulously clean — cats will find creative alternatives if they dislike it.

🦳 Key Notes Bi-annual vet visits are the standard of care from here on. Blood panel once a year helps catch hyperthyroidism, kidney disease, and diabetes early — all common in cats this age. Weight loss despite good appetite is a red flag. Lower litter box sides if jumping becomes difficult. Keep routines as consistent as possible; mature cats are creatures of deep habit.

Senior / Elder (11+ years)

Cats at this age can live well into their late teens. Shorter, gentler everything — play, walks, grooming. Comfort, warmth, and familiarity matter most. Some cognitive decline is normal.

🌙 Key Notes Bi-annual vet visits with blood work are essential. Arthritis is very common and very treatable — watch for reluctance to jump, stiffness after rest, or missing the litter box. Provide ramps or steps to favorite spots. Keep food, water, and litter on the same floor. Nighttime yowling, disorientation, or staring into space may indicate feline cognitive dysfunction — talk to your vet. These years are precious. 🐾
